The reason all top songs sound the same.

From 2000 to 2019, Spotify has kept detailed records of its most popular songs. By analyzing the attributes of these songs, we aim to discover why these top songs are beginning to all sound the same, and whether there's any chance of a new type of song taking the crown.

How influential are these genre tags? Do they even matter?

Each song has several key attributes that are unique to that song.
These attributes are computed, and set to range between 0 and 1.
By averaging these attributes, we can discover trends that exists in specific genres.

Danceability describes how suitable a track is for dancing based on a combination of musical elements including tempo, rhythm stability, beat strength, and overall regularity.
Valence describes the musical positiveness conveyed by a track. Tracks with high valence sound more positive (e.g. happy, cheerful, euphoric), while tracks with low valence sound more negative (e.g. sad, depressed, angry).
Liveness detects the presence of an audience in the recording. Higher liveness values represent an increased probability that the track was performed live.

Acousticness is a confidence measure of whether the track is acoustic.
Speechiness detects the presence of spoken words in a track. The more exclusively speech-like the recording (e.g. talk show, audio book, poetry), the closer to 1.0.
Energy represents a perceptual measure of intensity and activity.

The two most popular genres, Pop and HipHop, are remarkably similar in their attributes.
